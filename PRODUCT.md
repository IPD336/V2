# Product

## Register

product

Brand override applies for marketing surfaces (landing page, campaign pages).

## Users

Working professionals, students, and self-taught learners who want to trade expertise instead of paying for courses or tutoring. Time-constrained, goal-oriented, and looking for genuine human connection in their learning — not another content platform.

- **As a professional**: you have skills to offer and gaps to fill. You want structured, reciprocal learning with peers who respect your time.
- **As a learner**: you're early in your journey or exploring a new domain. You want mentorship without the formality or cost of traditional education.
- **As a community member**: you want recognition, reputation, and a sense of belonging. Leagues, badges, and leaderboards make your contributions visible.

## Product Purpose

SkillSwap is a peer-to-peer skill exchange platform where professionals connect, swap expertise, and grow together. Instead of paying for courses or tutoring, you trade your skills directly with others — you teach what you know and learn what you don't.

No money. No subscriptions. Just genuine knowledge exchange between people who want to grow together.

Success looks like: thousands of completed swaps, measurable skill growth, an active community where reputation carries weight, and a product so intuitive that barriers to exchange disappear.

## Brand Personality

**Warm · Confident · Community-driven**

Approachable authority — like a trusted senior colleague who's genuinely excited to help you level up. Professional without being corporate. Energetic without being juvenile. The brand feels like a workshop, not a lecture hall.

- Voice: direct, encouraging, human. No hype, no jargon.
- Tone: varies by context — celebratory on completion, clear on instructions, warm on empty states.
- Emotional goals: belonging, progress, trust.

## Anti-references

SkillSwap should NOT look like:

- **Generic SaaS cream/warmth** — sand beige backgrounds, ghost cards with soft shadows, glassmorphism, gradient text. The start-up monoculture.
- **Tiny uppercase eyebrow labels** over every section ("ABOUT", "FEATURES", "PROCESS") — the 2023-era AI tell.
- **Numbered section markers (01 / 02 / 03)** as default scaffolding — numbers earn their place only when order carries information.
- **Identical card grids** — same-sized cards with icon + heading + text, repeated.
- **Side-stripe borders** — the `border-left: 4px solid X` accent on cards or list items.
- **Dark "because tools look cool dark"** — dark mode serves actual use cases (ambient light, reading comfort), not aesthetics.

## Design Principles

1. **Community serves the product, not the other way around.** Every design choice answers: does this make the exchange easier, faster, or more trustworthy? Decoration follows function.

2. **Progress must be visible.** Gamification (leagues, badges, leaderboards) is earned reputation, not decoration. Every interaction should leave the user knowing where they stand.

3. **Warmth without cliché.** The palette is warm by conviction, not by default. Cream backgrounds are the brand's signature, not the AI's — supported by intentional color strategy, not warm-tinted neutrals.

4. **Typography is the interface.** PT Serif heads the hierarchy with editorial authority; PT Sans carries the body with clarity; PT Mono marks metadata. The fonts do the work — no decorative chrome needed.

5. **Trust through transparency.** Profiles, reviews, ratings, match scores — every piece of information that builds trust is surfaced early, never buried.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

General best practices: 4.5:1 body text contrast, proper focus-visible states, reduced motion support via `prefers-reduced-motion`, responsive layout at all breakpoints. No specific WCAG level target beyond industry standard.
